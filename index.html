The problem HR sees first

Capable teams stall for human reasons,
not technical ones.

You've promoted your best performers. You've run the skills training. Yet the friction persists — because most leadership problems are actually self-awareness problems in disguise.

01 / Communication

Messages sent ≠ messages received

Leaders default to the style that works on people like themselves. Cross-functional trust erodes quietly — until it shows up in missed handoffs, rework and "alignment meetings" that align nothing.

02 / Engagement

Managers set the emotional weather

Gallup attributes about 70% of the variance in team engagement to the manager. A leader who doesn't understand their own patterns under stress passes that stress straight down the org chart.

03 / Succession

High-potentials plateau at "presence"

The feedback is always the same: "needs more executive presence." But presence isn't taught in a slide deck — it's built through self-awareness, self-acceptance and deliberate practice.

The method

Self-awareness is a leadership system, not a personality quiz.

The Enneagram maps the motivation behind behaviour — why a leader micromanages under stress, avoids conflict, or over-commits their team. That's what makes it actionable where generic profiling tools stop at description.

01

Self-Awareness

Each leader discovers their core motivations, fears and default patterns — including the specific behaviours that show up under pressure. Assessment plus debrief, so the insight lands personally, not generically.

02

Self-Acceptance

Leaders stop fighting their wiring and start working with it. Defensiveness drops, feedback conversations open up, and teams get a shared, blame-free language for their differences.

03

Self-Mastery

Insight becomes behaviour: adapting communication styles, catching stress patterns early, influencing without forcing authority. Each participant leaves with a personalised growth roadmap.

FAQ

Questions HR & L&D leaders ask us.

Is the Enneagram rigorous enough for a corporate setting?
We use it as a facilitation and coaching framework, not a hiring or assessment instrument. Its value in a leadership context is practical: it gives teams a shared, blame-free language for motivation and stress behaviour, which is where most workplace friction actually lives. Every engagement pairs the framework with real business situations your leaders are navigating now.
How is this different from MBTI or DISC workshops we've already run?
Most typing tools describe what people do. The Enneagram maps why — the core motivations and fears driving behaviour, and crucially, how each type shifts under stress. That's what makes it coachable: leaders don't just get a label, they get a specific, observable pattern to work on.
What group sizes and formats do you support?
The 9 Styles of Leadership workshop is a half-day (3-hour) in-person session, ideal for department heads, managers and high-potential leaders — typically 8–25 participants. Keynotes scale to any audience size. Executive coaching is 1-on-1, in-person in Singapore or over video, in 3- or 6-session arcs. Multi-cohort rollouts can be scoped on the discovery call.
How do you handle confidentiality in executive coaching?
Coaching conversations are confidential between coach and coachee. Where HR sponsors the engagement, we agree upfront on a check-in structure that shares themes and progress — never session content — so you get visibility without compromising the trust that makes coaching work.
How is pricing structured?
Corporate engagements are scoped and quoted per programme, based on format, participant numbers and follow-up depth. You'll receive a clear proposal after the discovery call — suitable for procurement and L&D budget processes.
Can this fit into an existing leadership development programme?
Yes — 9 Styles of Leadership is often run as a module inside broader HiPo or leadership curricula, and executive coaching frequently follows a workshop as targeted support for selected leaders. We'll design around what you already have.
